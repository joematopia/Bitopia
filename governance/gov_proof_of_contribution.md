# 🏛 $GOV: The Meritocratic Reputation Token

**Classification:** Non-Transferable Soul-Bound Token | **Issuance:** Proof-of-Contribution (PoC) Only

## 1. The Core Philosophy
In terrestrial systems, capital buys influence (1 Dollar = 1 Vote). In Bitopia, influence cannot be bought; it must be earned. **$GOV** represents a Citizen's verifiable contribution to the Network State. It is soul-bound to prevent whales from purchasing political power with **$SOV** or other external assets.

## 2. Minting Logic (Proof-of-Contribution)
$GOV is minted automatically by the **dasOS kernel** when a Citizen’s Axiom Hardware or digital action provides a verifiable, cryptographically-proven benefit to the State.

**Eligible Contributions & Weighting:**
* **Engineering/Code Commits:** High weight (e.g., merging core logic to `blocks` or `dasOS`).
* **Axiom Node Operation:** High weight (Maintaining high-uptime physical energy attestation via Axiom-C or Axiom-4 hardware).
* **Judicial Arbitration:** Medium weight (serving as a fair node in dispute resolution).
* **Mathematical/Scientific Proofs:** High weight (optimizing thermodynamic capture or ZK-identity efficiency).
* **Network Security/Debugging:** Medium weight (identifying vulnerabilities in the consensus layer).

## 3. The Madison Protocol: Quadratic Voting
To protect the minority from the tyranny of the majority, all systemic governance votes utilizing $GOV are processed through a Quadratic Voting algorithm.

**The Mathematical Constraint:** The voting power ($V$) of a Citizen is equal to the square root of their $GOV reputation ($S$).
$$V = \sqrt{S}$$

*Example:* A Citizen with 100 $GOV holds 10x the influence of a Citizen with 1 $GOV. This forces leaders to build consensus rather than dictating terms.
